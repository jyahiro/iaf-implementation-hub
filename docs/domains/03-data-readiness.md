# Domain III: Data

## Executive Summary
Domain III establishes data readiness for analytics execution. Per the INFORMS Analytics Framework, this domain identifies and prioritizes data needs, analyzes data sources and structures, plans and executes data acquisition, prepares and validates data, evaluates quality, and documents findings. Domain III is complete when data is demonstrably fit for purpose and the data foundation is governable, secure, and reproducible.

## Operational Alignment
Data readiness supports enterprise strategy by ensuring analytics decisions are based on reliable, governed, and well-documented information assets. This domain advances organizational decision-support maturity through standardized data stewardship practices, transparency of data lineage, and disciplined data quality controls that reduce operational and compliance risk.

## IAF Standard Checklist (Readiness Assessment)
Before advancing to Domain IV, the project lead must confirm:

- Priority data requirements and authoritative sources are identified and approved.
- Data management planning addresses access, stewardship, privacy, and security controls.
- Required data is acquired and prepared (cleaned, harmonized, transformed, merged, validated).
- Data quality and key data relationships are assessed and documented.
- Business and analytics problem statements are validated against observed data realities.

## Artifact Requirements
A project is Domain III compliant only when the following artifacts are complete and current:

- Data Requirements and Source Inventory.
- Data Management Plan.
- Data Acquisition and Preparation Log.
- Data Quality and Relationship Assessment Report.
- Data Findings Report, including updates to business and analytics problem statements.
