# Legal Guardrails

All implementation guidance in the IAF Implementation Hub is governed by applicable law, policy, and agency directives. For U.S. federal contexts, the primary statutory guardrail is the [Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018 (Evidence Act)](https://www.congress.gov/115/plaws/publ435/PLAW-115publ435.pdf), Public Law 115-435.

## Governing Reference

- **Statute:** [Foundations for Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018 (Evidence Act)](https://www.congress.gov/115/plaws/publ435/PLAW-115publ435.pdf)
- **Public Law:** Pub. L. 115-435
- **Date Enacted:** January 14, 2019
- **Primary Source:** [Congress.gov Public Law 115-435](https://www.congress.gov/115/plaws/publ435/PLAW-115publ435.pdf)

## Implementation Guardrail Standard

For all domains, templates, and services in this Hub:

- Evidence-building activities must be documented, decision-relevant, and reviewable.
- Data use must align with approved governance, privacy, confidentiality, and security controls.
- Artifacts must support transparency, auditability, and reproducibility.
- Roles and approvals must be explicit for accountability.
- Outputs should support lawful, ethical, and policy-aligned decision-making.

## Scope and Use

This Hub is agency-agnostic and can support federal, state, local, and public-adjacent organizations. Where stricter legal or policy requirements apply, those requirements supersede this guidance.
